🌸 Monument of 21st Century Sakura Tree — Gasubashi 📍 Maps
YOUR backyard sakura — and one of Ota City's finest. This is the "21st Century Sakura" green space along the Tama River embankment, officially listed by Ota City as a top cherry blossom destination. It's a 15-minute walk from your base — no train needed.

Why it's special: ~120 Somei Yoshino trees line the Tama River cycling path along the embankment. Unlike famous spots (no crowds), the trees form a long corridor with wide-open river views, wind off the water carrying petals, cyclists and joggers weaving through blossoms — a pure, authentic Tokyo local sakura experience. At full bloom today, the petals should be drifting onto the path.

Continue south along the embankment toward Tamagawadai Park (another 15min walk) where 300+ cherry trees cover the hillside park with views of the river.
📍 How to get there: Walk north from your base (~15min) toward the Tama River embankment near the Canon Building / Shimo-maruko Bridge. Follow the cycling path. FREE. No transit. Open 24hrs.
📸 Insta360 Ace Pro 2 shots here:
— Ultra-wide tunnel shot: Stand in the middle of the cycling path facing the cherry tree corridor. Activate the ultra-wide lens and walk slowly toward camera on Explorer grip — gets the full canopy arch overhead.
— Low grip river shot: Hold Explorer grip at ankle height while walking along the embankment, river on one side, trees on the other. AI will stabilise beautifully.
— Petal drift hyperlapse: Set camera on ground or prop on railing aimed up through branches. 5-min hyperlapse as petals drift. This is the keeper shot.
✓ FREE · 15min walk🌸 Zero Crowds

📸 Insta360 Ace Pro 2 — Tokyo Street Photography Guide

📷
Your setup: Insta360 Ace Pro 2 + Explorer grip. This is actually one of the best combinations for Japan street photography — the wide-angle is non-threatening (people don't feel "pointed at"), the AI stabilisation handles walking shots perfectly, and the Explorer grip gives you creative low/high angles that traditional cameras can't. Japan is also extremely permissive for public photography in parks, streets and festivals.
🌸 Sakura Photography
Tama River Gasubashi (Fri morning)
Hold grip at ground level pointing up through cherry trees as you walk. The sky + blossoms + wide-angle = spectacular. Also try: set camera on the embankment railing, step back, let petals drift across the lens.
Nakameguro at night (Fri evening)
Reflection shot from bridge: grip at railing height, angled 45° downward — captures lanterns + cherry canopy + water reflection simultaneously. The AI low-light handles this beautifully.
Chidorigafuchi (Sat morning)
Walk the 700m promenade slowly with grip held arm's length ahead — the cherry tunnel overhead is a perfect wide-angle corridor shot. Try a hyperlapse of the full walk.
Ueno Park (Mon early)
Crowd + blossoms together. Hold Explorer grip above head in the main avenue — captures the scale of 1,000+ trees with people below. Set to burst mode to capture candid moments.
🏙️ Tokyo Street Photography
Shibuya Scramble (Mon after training)
Stand in the centre of the scramble crossing mid-walk. Hold Explorer grip at hip height, wide-angle, and walk across. Captures the chaos from within it. Also try from a konbini window above — look for 7-Eleven on the 2nd floor overlooking the crossing.
Akihabara electric strip (Mon)
Walk the main Chuo-dori with grip held out front, neon signs and anime banners everywhere. The wide lens catches the full visual overload. Shoot at dusk when neon kicks in.
Nakano Broadway 3F (Sat)
Tight corridors of glass watch cases + overhead fluorescent light = great documentary shot. Keep grip low at waist level — less intrusive, more candid feel.
Yurikamome monorail to Odaiba (Mon)
Sit at the front window seat (no driver — driverless train). Grip against the glass, wide-angle. The elevated track over Tokyo Bay with Rainbow Bridge is a cinematic shot, especially at sunset heading to Odaiba.
🥋 Competition + BJJ Shots
Competition day (Sun) — Komazawa Park
Wide-angle of the venue exterior with cherry blossoms framing the 1964 Olympics gymnasium — a very special shot. Capture the young warrior warming up in his GI with sakura visible through the windows if possible.
Mat-level shots
Set camera on the edge of the mat (if allowed) with wide-angle. Even 30 seconds of competition footage in ultra-wide is something he'll treasure forever. Check with tournament organizers first.
Post-match emotion
The Insta360's burst mode is perfect for capturing the moment after a match — win or lose. The wide-angle catches everything: the handshake, the coach, the parent, the exhaustion, the joy. These are the frames that matter.
Training at Carpe Diem (Fri)
Ask if you can film a short clip of the drilling session. Mat-level angle on Explorer grip during a roll sequence is documentary gold. Pair with the wide exterior shot of Jiyugaoka for context.
⌚ Watch Hunt + Shopping
Nakano Broadway approach (Sat)
Walk from Nakano station toward Broadway — overhead shop signs, packed shopping street, locals going about their day. Hold grip at chest height walking naturally. The AI captures it all without you needing to look at the screen.
Watch case detail shots
The Ace Pro 2's macro-capable wide lens can get incredibly close to watch faces in display cases. Get the glass reflection + your face + the watch all in one wide frame — a very "collector" shot. Ask permission at FireKids (Anton/Kyara are known to be friendly).
Harajuku Cat Street (Mon)
The narrow backstreet between Harajuku and Omotesando (Ura-Harajuku) where Studio D'Artisan and sneaker boutiques are. Excellent street photography: narrow lane, curated storefronts, fashion-conscious locals. Hold grip low at hip for a "street level" perspective.
🎮 Kids + Action Shots
Gashapon machines (Mon)
Point Ace Pro 2 at the capsule machine from below as the young warrior inserts coins and turns the handle. Ultra-wide captures the whole wall of machines + his excitement simultaneously — no need to zoom.
Nintendo Tokyo (Mon)
Get him standing next to a life-size Mario/Link statue — hold grip at the statue's chest height, wide-angle captures both him and the full statue without stepping back far. Perfect for the cramped store space.
Unicorn Gundam transformation (Mon)
Set the Ace Pro 2 on burst mode or video during the hourly transformation. Wide-angle from ground level pointed up captures the full 20m robot against the sky. If you can, set the camera on the ground directly below it — the ultra-wide distortion makes it look 100m tall.
teamLab Borderless (Sat/Mon)
The immersive rooms were designed to be photographed. Wide-angle + slow movement with Explorer grip = the rooms "wrap around" you in the footage. Set to video mode and just walk through slowly — no need to think about framing.
💡 Insta360 Ace Pro 2 Settings
For sakura in daylight
4K 30fps · Ultra-wide lens · Horizon Steady ON · Auto exposure. The cherry blossoms are bright whites/pinks — tap to expose for the sky slightly to avoid blown highlights.
For night scenes (Nakameguro lanterns)
4K 24fps · PureVideo night mode ON · ISO limit to 1600 · Steady+ ON. The Ace Pro 2's f/1.8 aperture handles lantern light beautifully — don't bump ISO too high or it gets noisy.
For action / street
2.7K 60fps (smooth slow-mo in edit) · AI tracking OFF for street candids (less conspicuous). Explorer grip at waist height, camera facing forward — you can shoot without looking like you're shooting.
Explorer grip technique
Extend the grip fully (selfie pole length) and hold it OUT to the side at arm's length — you get a completely different perspective than everyone else's phone shots. Rotate the camera upward 45° for overhead cherry canopy shots without raising your arm above your head.

💡 Japan photography etiquette: Shrines (Meiji Jingu) ask you not to photograph prayer areas or inside the main hall. Asking permission before photographing individuals is appreciated though rarely enforced outdoors. Competition venues may restrict filming — check with organizers. All outdoor public spaces and street scenes are completely fine to photograph freely.
